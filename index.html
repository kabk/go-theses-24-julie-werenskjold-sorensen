Mother Earth or Mother Nature. There are countless of examples of cultures and Mythologies, which have an Earth Goddess or God, however a red thread is that the Earth is most commonly depicted as a Goddess. A woman. And we can of course not forget about the The Goddess Gaia from Greek Mythology, called, the mother earth or the earth moth (12), and there are many more out there.

Nature, the word itself, is derived from the Latin word natura, which in ancient archetype of the mother is a natural caretaker, maintaining and sustaining life through emotional, physical and spiritual support, healing and nurturing. ‚Äú‚Ä¶considered to be natural to or characteristics of a woman,‚Ä¶‚Äù (3). But whether we choose to believe the stories of the bible or not, there have been people on earth for thousands of years, and we are brought up to believe the first tool of humankind was the spear. The tool of hunting and killing. The commonly accepted fact: a male hero , bringing home the pray and the gasp worthy story of the hunt. In her text The Carrier Bag Theory of Fiction, Ursula K. We have become greatly separated from our ancestors from back when we were a group of gathers, and this is a mark which is rooted deeply within us. How is it women have played such crucial roles of survival, and that the exact skill of gathering and harvesting, was eventually what went on to be one of the causes for mass-hysteria and the witch hunts through Europe?
